---
title: Turkish's Verb "To Hate" Requires the Ablative (-den/dan) Case 
date: 2026-03-12
tags: turkish, language
---
### _Nefret etmeyi neden istiyoruz._  

(See [here]("/accusative-istmek.html") for why _etmeyi_ has to take the accustative case.)  

Consider the following sentence: "I hate going to school." A simple and trivial statement, but the Turkish translation may throw you off some.   

### _Okula gitmekten nefret ediyorum_.  

Here's the deal-eeyo. The verb _nefret etmek_ takes the ablative case, which is used to indicate "from" something. The literal translation then is "I hate from going to school," because apparently the Turks view hate as a state you acquire, passively, and not an active feeling towards something.  

I may start saying stuff like "I hate from grapefruit." Yes, grapefruit made me this way.
