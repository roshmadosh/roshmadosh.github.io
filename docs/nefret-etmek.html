Turkish's Verb "To Hate" Requires the Ablative (-den/dan) Case

2026-03-12 turkishlanguage

Nefret etmeyi neden istiyoruz.

(See here for why etmeyi has to take the accustative case.)

Consider the following sentence: "I hate going to school." While I can end the post here, the Turkish translation may throw you off some.

Okula gitmekten nefret ediyorum.

If you were thrown off like I was by the -ten ending, here's the deal-eeyo.

The verb nefret etmek takes the ablative case, which is used to indicate "from" something. The literal translation then is "I hate from going to school."

Interesting because this makes hate seem like some general state you acquire passively, rather than an active feeling towards something. It makes hate as a concept seem less dangerous, I guess. Not a bad thing.
